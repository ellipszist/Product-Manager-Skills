## What the Market Intelligence Suite Is (architecture, not just a list)

Fourteen new skills under `theme: market-intelligence` (also a **new marketplace category** of the same name), plus two upgraded skills. The structure matters more than the roster:

- **`autonomous-investigation`** (Workflow) is the suite's **protocol skill** — the counterpart to `workshop-facilitation`, but for skills where *the world* holds the context instead of the user. Its seven-clause contract: question budget (proceed on labeled assumptions when spent/silent), search-plan gate, **Fact / Inference / Assumption** evidence labels (the library's canonical vocabulary — Dean chose this over Confirmed/Inferred/Projected/Unknown), do-not-invent lists, Just Enough Mode, **stable diffable output schemas**, exactly-4-option Final Step. Plus: the Confidence Stacking Rule (1 channel = watch item, 2 = hypothesis, 3+ = actionable, conflict = dig), "announcements are intent until funding/hiring corroborate," and SCIP guardrails.
- **`intelligence-collection-disciplines`** (Component) is the **backbone**: eight intel disciplines (OSINT, FININT, GEOINT/DEMOINT, TECHINT, HUMINT, SIGINT, MASINT, All-Source Fusion) with free/paid source tables, signal→inference chains, the bottom-up TAM/SAM/SOM recipe, fusion cadence, and the artifact-mapping table. **Do not split it into eight skills** — fusion is the point.
- **`intel-discipline-advisor`** (Interactive) is the backbone's **Component + Interactive pairing** (Dean's preferred pedagogic model): 3 adaptive questions → discipline mix + cadence + executing skill, teaching the mapping-table logic as it routes, with an honest off-ramp to discovery/win-loss.
- **The chain** (all Workflow, each consumes the prior's stable schema): `market-landscape-scan` → `competitive-research-snapshot` → `competitive-intel-watch` → `battle-card-builder`.
- **Strategy frameworks, evidence-cited:** `swot-analysis`, `porters-five-forces`, `ansoff-matrix`. Ansoff is deliberately cross-referenced with `organic-growth-advisor` as its investigation sibling (same Ansoff-shaped map; coaching there, evidence here) — **do not "deduplicate" them.**
- **Monitors/miners:** `voice-of-customer-miner`, `pricing-packaging-tracker`, `pestel-delta-monitor` (pairs with `pestel-analysis`; hard prerequisite on a baseline).
- **Umbrella:** `competitive-analysis-process` — six-step orchestration (from Dean's Competitive Analysis Playbook), delegating to the suite the way `discovery-process` delegates to discovery skills.
- **Upgrades:** `tam-sam-som-calculator` gained three entry modes (own numbers / guided interview / **autonomous research** with the GEOINT recipe + FININT capture rates); `company-intel` gained the Executive Signal Refresh rerun pattern (Then/Now diffs, exact-quote rule, **Dropped Language** section), source priority ladder, do-not-sanitize rule, product-org heuristics (CPO tenure, PM job postings as culture documents), and an optional distress read.

### Conventions the suite established (respect these in future skills)

1. **Investigation skills declare the protocol** — References names `autonomous-investigation` as governing; body states question budget, do-not-invent list, output schema ("do not reorder"), Final Step block.
2. **Free/paid two-column source tables** are the sanctioned house style for tool mentions (neutral, always a free path). This is NOT the same as vendor backlinks in templates, which we reject (see PR #15 below).
3. **Fact / Inference / Assumption** everywhere. Unknowns are a gaps list, not a fourth label.
4. **Output schemas are stable contracts** — diffability enables the monitors; "improving" a schema between runs is a regression.

### Customer anonymization protocol (CRITICAL, permanent)
No Productside customer names — and no example scenario keeping the recognizable *shape* of a real engagement — may appear in the repo. Examples use bracket placeholders (`[TARGET]`, `[Competitor A]`) or clearly fictional companies in rotated domains. `company-intel`'s examples were fictionalized on Jul 16 (Parker Hannifin/Emerson/Honeywell/Novo Nordisk → **Helix Motion Systems, Northfield Automation, Corvid Industrial, Brightwater Biologics**). Real names only for canonical public framework cases (e.g., Amazon for PR/FAQ). Before committing new content, sweep new files (`grep -E '\b[A-Z][a-zA-Z]+ (Inc|Corp|GmbH|AG|Ltd|LLC|Group)\b'` plus any names Dean supplies in-session). **Never commit a customer denylist to the repo — the list itself would be the leak.** Podcast transcripts also stay out of the repo (CLAUDE.md's Phase 8 pointer to `research/product_porch_transcript.*.md` refers to files deliberately not committed — that's correct, not a bug).
